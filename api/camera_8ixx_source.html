15 /**
16 * @brief Computes the 4x4 perspective projection matrix based on the specified
17 * field of view, aspect ratio, and near and far clipping planes.
18 *
19 * This method returns a matrix suitable for projecting 3D points onto a
20 * 2D screen from the perspective of a camera. The perspective transformation
21 * maps 3D points inside the specified viewing frustum to normalized device
22 * coordinates, which are then clipped and transformed to screen space.
23 *
24 * @param fovY Field of view in the Y direction, specified in radians.
25 * @param aspect The aspect ratio of the view, defined as width divided by height.
26 * @param zNear The distance to the near clipping plane. Must be greater than zero.
27 * @param zFar The distance to the far clipping plane. Must be greater than zNear.
28 *
29 * @return A 4x4 matrix representing the perspective projection transformation.
30 *
31 * @see https://thorsten.suckow-homberg.de/docs/articles/computer-graphics/from-camera-to-clip-space-derivation-of-the-projection-matrices
32 */
33 mat4f perspective(float fovY, float aspect, float zNear, float zFar) noexcept;

35 /**
36 * @brief Returns the 4x4 lookAt-matrix for transforming world coordinates
37 * to camera space.
38 * The method creates the camera coordinate system based on the
39 * parameters eye, i.e. the vantage point in world space, the camera's
40 * up direction and center, representing the point of interest.
41 * Note that the returned matrix is essentially a composition of
42 * a change of coordinates matrix P(C <- W) and a translation
43 * to the origin, i.e. the camera is sitting at the origin (0, 0, 0)
44 * looking down the negative z-axis, i.e. conceptually
45 *
46 * P(W<-C) * T(-eye)
47 *
48 * The 4x4 matrix can then be used for computing the perspective projection
49 * for creating the clip space
50 *
51 * clip = P * V * W * M
52 *
53 * @param eye
54 * @param center
55 * @param up
56 * @return
57 */
58 mat4f lookAt(const vec3f& eye, const vec3f& center, const vec3f& up) noexcept;
