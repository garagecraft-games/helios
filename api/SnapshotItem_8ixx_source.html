16 /**
17 * @brief Structure for representing a snapshot item of a renderable's object
18 * state at a specific point in time.
19 *
20 * The structure bundles a weak pointer to a Renderable and a pre-calculated
21 * worldMatrix, serving as a lightweight DTO to be used with a `Snapshot`.
22 *
23 * A SnapshotItem captures the essential information to render an item
24 * without the need to traverse the entire Scene Graph during the
25 * rendering phase.
26 * The worldMatrix is a copy of the SceneNode's worldMatrix at the time of this
27 * creation, ensuring that its value remains constant, even if the transformation
28 * of the SceneNode should change during rendering.
29 *
30 * Note that for the Renderable a weak pointer is stored to allow for checking its
31 * existence in the render pass. While internal properties of the Renderable
32 * might change while this SnapshotItem is processed, the worldMatrix is immutable
33 * and reflects the transformation state at the moment the Snapshot was taken.
34 *
35 * Thus, as a precondition, we assume that no changes to the Renderable are
36 * made while the Renderable "exists" as a weak ptr in a particular Snapshot
37 * of a scene that would lead to invalidating its rendering. We rely on the
38 * state information available within the Renderable as a reflection of its
39 * state when the Snapshot was taken.
40 *
41 * @todo we're intentionally preferring stability over performance by using
42 * weak_ptr instead of raw pointers. This leaves room for (performance) improvements
43 * in later iterations.
44 * The Renderable as a weak pointer can later on be further implemented into
45 * a pure DTO, storing only scalar values that get send directly to the underlying
46 * rendering backend (e.g. vaos, vbos etc.).
47 */
